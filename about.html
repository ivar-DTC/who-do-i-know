About the Neighbor Network

A simple tool for mapping the connections that already exist in our community.

What it is

You already know more of your neighbors than you think.

The Neighbor Network is a tool that helps Woodstock DTC volunteers identify which voters in town they already have a relationship with — whether that's someone they know well, or simply a familiar face from around town.

You review your neighbors one household at a time, working through a street before moving on to the next. For each home, you say whether you know the people there, recognize them, or have no connection at all. That's it.

How a review works

Street by street, household by household.

1
Pick your name to begin The app pulls up the households waiting for your review and starts you on a street. You can also tap "Pick a road" to jump to a specific street you know well.
2
Take each household in turn You'll see everyone who lives at an address together. If you don't know anyone there, one tap marks the whole household and moves you on. If you do, open it up and answer for each person.
3
Finish a street, then the next Your progress is shown for the street you're on. Complete it and the app rolls straight into another — your answers save as you go.

Already reviewed someone and need to change your answer — or want to update one person without going through a whole street? Use Update individual response to search for anyone by name or address and set how you know them.

The three responses

Three simple responses for every neighbor.

✓
Know them You have a real relationship with this person — a neighbor, a friend, someone you'd stop to talk to.
◎
Know of them You recognize the name or face — maybe from around town, a local event, or a mutual connection — but don't know them personally.
✕
Don't know them No connection at all. That's a perfectly valid answer — and just as useful to know.
Why it matters

A warm conversation is worth a dozen cold ones.

When a DTC member reaches out to a voter they already know, that conversation is warmer and more likely to make a difference. People are far more receptive to hearing from a neighbor they trust than from a stranger at the door.

By mapping existing connections across our volunteer network, we can make sure that when it matters — before an election, during a campaign, or just in an important local conversation — the right person is doing the reaching out.

Your responses help us understand who in Woodstock is already one conversation away.
